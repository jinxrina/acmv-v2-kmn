Frequently Asked Questions

What is ACMV and why is it important?

ACMV stands for Air-Conditioning and Mechanical Ventilation. It's crucial for maintaining optimal indoor temperature, humidity, and air quality in Singapore's hot and humid climate. Proper ACMV system design ensures comfort, energy efficiency, and compliance with building regulations.

How accurate are the calculations provided by this tool?

Our calculations are based on Singapore's SS553 Code of Practice for Air-Conditioning and Mechanical Ventilation in Buildings. They provide a reliable estimation suitable for preliminary design and planning. For final design, we always recommend consulting with a professional ACMV engineer to account for specific building characteristics and requirements.

What factors affect cooling load calculations?

Several factors influence cooling load calculations including: building orientation, building envelope (walls, roof, windows), occupancy, lighting and equipment heat generation, ventilation requirements, and local climate conditions. Our calculator takes all these factors into account based on Singapore's specific climate conditions.

What's the difference between BTU, kW, HP, and RT?

These are different units for measuring cooling capacity:
- BTU/h (British Thermal Unit per hour): Common unit for air conditioners
- kW (Kilowatt): Standard metric unit (1 kW = 3,412 BTU/h)
- HP (Horsepower): Often used for residential units (1 HP ≈ 9,000 BTU/h)
- RT (Refrigeration Ton): Used for larger systems (1 RT = 12,000 BTU/h)
Our calculator provides results in multiple units for your convenience.

How often should I recalculate my cooling requirements?

It's advisable to recalculate cooling requirements when significant changes occur, such as building renovations, changes in occupancy, addition of heat-generating equipment, or when experiencing consistent cooling issues. For commercial buildings, periodic reviews every 3-5 years are recommended to ensure optimal system performance and energy efficiency.
